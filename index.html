My Process

My teaching philosophy is simple: Every student’s learning style is unique; teach in the style that best befits each student.

Whether you’re a beginner learning how to read notes or practicing for your final Master’s recital, I create a fun, structured learning environment with positive reinforcement and accountability so you can continuously improve your playing without stress. If you’re a fast learner, be careful—I move quickly!

Through demonstration, accompaniment (on the piano or cello), and my repository of exciting learning activities I’ve cultivated over the years, I’m here to help you build a strong playing foundation while guiding you toward your musical goals.

FAQ's

How long are the lessons?

0 minutes or 1 hour—you pick what works best for you! I typically recommend 1-hour lessons so we have more time to dig in and get more granular into your material. But if you only have time to squeeze in 30 minutes for your busy schedule, we’ll always get a lot done.

How often should I have lessons?

Most of my students like to have weekly lessons. Fewer are bi-weekly, and even fewer are monthly. If at any time you feel that you need more or fewer lessons, schedule as to adjust to your pace.

Do I have to bring my own cello?

For both in-person and Zoom lessons, please bring your own instrument. If you don’t have one, contact me through the contact form below and I’ll help guide you to the right place.

Where are in-person lessons?

I live near Bancroft and McCord. If this is your first in-person lesson, please contact me via the contact form below so we can discuss the in-person logistics.

What instruments do you teach?

Although I’m a classically trained cellist, I teach piano up to an intermediate advanced level.

Do you provide piano accompaniment?

Yes! I play piano accompaniment at recitals for all stringed instruments. Please contact me below to discuss details.

Do I have to buy my own sheet music?

Yes. All my students purchase their own sheet music. However, I do have a large collection of sheet music in case you forget to bring yours. Don’t worry about which ones to get—I can make suggestions based on your level and we’ll work through it together.

In-person lessons FAQ's

Do I have to bring a music stand?

Nope! I have one here for you.

Do I pay with card or cash?

I ask that you please bring cash to each lesson. I don’t always have exact change, so please try to bring the exact amount if possible.

Do I have to wear a mask?

I ask you kindly to wear a mask during the lesson unless you are fully vaccinated, as I teach several students a day.

Zoom FAQ's

How does this work?

I’ve taught hundreds of students over Zoom and Skype during the pandemic. It works just the same as in-person lessons. However, the connection quality sometimes isn’t as good over the internet so I do advise you to take in-person lessons if possible.

How do I pay?

When choosing your lesson length and time slot, there will be an option to pay online with either PayPal or credit card.

Will you call me?

Yes, I will start the meeting or call you when the lesson time begins.

How much do you charge?

My lessons are charged based on the length of time. 30-minute lessons are $45, and 1-hour lessons are $80. If it will be a financial burden for you, please contact me below so we can discuss it. I hope for every student to have an opportunity to learn cello.
